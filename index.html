NEW VIDEO TECH ISN'T READY FOR STREAMING.

Latency

Wired and wireless networks are capacity-constrained. 4K UHD VR video uses 30-40 Mbps, yet even the best fiber-optic, DOCSIS 3.x, 4G+/5G networks can only achieve ~20 Mbps. This will only get worse as newer VR camera rigs output more demanding VR video streams such as 6K or 8K.

Resolution Demand

To achieve the fully immersive experience that 360 VR, 4K, 6K and even 8K resolution can bring, there is a lot of work to be done to make these formats functional on the hardware capacity of today's viewing devices and existing telecoms network bandwidth speeds, without sacrificing quality.

Poor UX

Due to huge file sizes and streaming demands, existing 360 VR and high resolution video can only be played on wired headsets with in-built processing power. For the web, and for untethered headsets, videos are pixellated, suffer huge buffering and startup delays, and have streaming latency causing nausea. This causes poor experience.

Meet Streem

Our Story

We are a team of machine learning and perceptual quality video bandwidth reduction researchers. After watching our first 360 video we were hooked. But we knew that for 360 VR to be feasible on existing networks, someone would have to build the enabling technologies. We wanted to apply our research in reducing video bandwidth without any change in perceptual quality to the emerging field of 360 VR, 4K HDR and even more demanding video.

Our Vision

Our vision is that in 5 years time, people will be watching high definition 8K 360 VR video, on next generation mobile VR headsets and mobile devices. To enable this future, VR content itself will need to have efficient processing pipelines that reduce file sizes and lower bitrates for transmission into networks, even if networks upgrade considerably. Our goal is to build the technology to do this.

Technology

Streem's field of vision rendering technology creates temporally cohesive geometries and maps these in real time to corresponding static UV coordinates. Combined with other techniques such as gaze prediction, forward error correction on UDP streaming protocols, object detection, and perceptual-quality based capped variable bit-rate adaptation, Streem can obtain bitrate reductions of up to 50% for a given 360 video. Our tech is scalable to even more demanding emerging file formats like 4K UHD and HDR.
